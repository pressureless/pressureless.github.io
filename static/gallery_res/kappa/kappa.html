1 Kappa curve

In geometry, the kappa curve or Gutschoven’s curve is a two-dimensional algebraic curve resembling the Greek letter ${\proselabel{kappa}{{κ}}}$ (kappa).

$$ {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)= {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2} \tag{1}\label{1}$$

The kappa curve was first studied by Gérard van Gutschoven around 1662. Other famous mathematicians who have studied it include Isaac Newton and Johann Bernoulli. Its tangents were first calculated by Isaac Barrow in the 17th century.

2 Tangents via infinitesimals

The tangent lines of the kappa curve can also be determined geometrically using differentials and the elementary rules of infinitesimal arithmetic. Suppose ${\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}}$ and ${\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}}$ are variables, while ${\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}}$ is taken to be a constant. From the definition of the kappa curve,

$$ {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)- {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}=0 \tag{7}\label{7}$$

Now, an infinitesimal change in our location must also change the value of the left hand side, so

$$ d\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)- {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)=0 \tag{8}\label{8}$$

Distributing the differential and applying appropriate rules,
$$ \begin{aligned} &d\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)\right)-d\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)=0 \\ &(2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} )\left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}(2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} +2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} )- {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} 2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} =0 \\ &\left(4 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{3}+2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right) d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} +\left(2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}-2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \right) d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} =0 \\ & {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} \left(2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right) d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} + {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}- {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2}\right) d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} =0 \\ &\frac{ {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} \left(2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)}{ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2}- {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}\right)}=\frac{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} } \end{aligned} \tag{9}\label{9}$$

3 Derivative

If we use the modern concept of a functional relationship ${\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} )$ and apply implicit differentiation, the slope of a tangent line to the kappa curve at a point $( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} , {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} )$ is :
$$ \begin{aligned} &2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} \left( {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}\right)+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2}\left(2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} +2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \frac{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} }\right)=2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \frac{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} } \\ &2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{3}+2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}+2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{3}=2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \frac{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} }-2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \frac{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} } \\ &4 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{3}+2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}=\left(2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} -2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} \right) \frac{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} } \\ &\frac{2 {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{3}+ {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} ^{2}}{ {\proselabel{kappa}{{a}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} - {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} ^{2} {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }=\frac{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{y}}} }{d {\proselabel{kappa}{{x}}} } \end{aligned} \tag{10}\label{10}$$
